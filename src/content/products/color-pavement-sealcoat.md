## Product Overview

Color pavement sealcoat is a water-based PMP micro-asphalt system for upgrading existing asphalt pavement with a colored, anti-skid, wear-resistant surface. It is suitable for pavement renewal projects that need clear visual guidance, stronger surface protection, and a cleaner construction process than hot colored asphalt overlays.

The system is often used as a lightweight pavement color renewal method on existing black asphalt. It improves appearance, friction, water resistance, and surface durability without removing and repaving the original pavement.

## Two-Component Micro-Asphalt System

The material is prepared from two components:

- **Component A:** water-based micro-asphalt emulsion.
- **Component B:** polymer micro-asphalt aggregate.

The standard mixing ratio is **A:B = 1:3**. The two components are blended with a hand mixer or mechanical mixer for approximately 1 minute until the mixture is uniform, with no visible settlement and no coarse particle feel. After mixing, the material is applied with dedicated micro-asphalt spray equipment.

## Layered Spray Structure

The pavement system uses a layered build-up rather than a single paint film:

1. **Base bonding layer:** promotes compatibility and adhesion to the existing asphalt surface.
2. **Wear-resistant middle layer:** provides body, flexibility, skid resistance, and abrasion resistance.
3. **Protective surface layer:** improves color retention, water resistance, and surface sealing.

For projects that require a thicker structure, the color system can be paired with a thin asphalt wearing course, such as a "2 cm thin asphalt layer + color micro-asphalt coating" solution.

## Color-Separated Construction Process

A typical two-color roadway process can divide a 7 m wide road into two 3.5 m lanes, then add a 10 cm center marking after both colors are complete. The same method can be adapted to bike lanes, bus lanes, pedestrian zones, parking areas, and scenic roads.

1. **Surface cleaning:** remove dust, soil, loose particles, oil contamination, and standing water. Heavily contaminated areas should be wire-brushed or pressure-washed. Apply an interface primer where needed.
2. **Masking and protection:** protect curbs, drainage structures, road edges, and the center color boundary with tape and masking paper. Use side shields during spray work to prevent overspray.
3. **First color spray:** spray the first color area after masking.
4. **Second color spray:** after the first color dries, mask and spray the second color area using the same method.
5. **Surface coat:** after the first full pass dries and the reaction completes, spray the second surface layer by color section.
6. **Marking:** after the surface layer dries and reacts, apply the center line or other road markings.
7. **Opening:** release the section after the coating is fully dry and the surface is stable.

## Technical Properties

- **Adhesion:** compatible with asphalt substrates and suitable for application soon after black asphalt pavement is completed.
- **Flexibility:** the material system balances strength and flexibility to reduce peeling risk.
- **Skid resistance:** textured micro-asphalt aggregate provides wet-condition friction; skid resistance target is not less than 0.50.
- **Wear resistance:** designed for slow-traffic systems and decorative pavement zones subject to foot, bicycle, and light vehicle traffic.
- **Water-based environmental profile:** low odor, water-based chemistry, and VOC reference limit not higher than 120 g/L.
- **Chemical resistance:** reference requirements include no blistering or peeling after water, oil, alkali, and acid exposure, with slight color change allowed.
- **Film appearance:** after drying, color and aggregate distribution should be uniform, with no cracking or aggregate shedding.
